Taras Hryhorovych Shevchenko

National hero and symbol of Ukraine

Here's a time line of Taras Shevchenko:

  • Taras Shevchenko was born on March 9, 1814.
  • In the spring of 1838 Karl Bryullov and Vasyl Zhukovsky decided to redeem the young poet from serfdom.
  • In the spring of 1845 the poet left for Ukraine, where he was going to live and work permanently. In 1846, while in Kyiv, he joined the secret Cyril and Methodius Society, which set itself the task of spreading education among the Ukrainian people.
  • On April 5, 1847, 33-year-old Shevchenko was arrested for his involvement in this society, and during a search, a manuscript of the poem "Dream" was found in his possession. The poet was sent to St. Petersburg, interrogations and investigations began.
  • At the beginning of 1857, the poet's friends obtained a royal order for his release.
  • On January 23, 1860, a new edition of "Kobzar" was published, although mutilated in some places by the tsarist censorship, but overall much more complete than "Kobzar" of 1840.
  • On September 4, 1860, the Council of the Academy of Arts granted Shevchenko the title of academic engraver. The poet worked a lot: he wrote poems, created new engravings, published and monitored the distribution of "Primary" - his last book.
  • On March 10, 1861, the life of T. Shevchenko broke off.
